Reading a CM360 Report Like a Media Planner, Not an Ops Person

Nine years across agency floors and enterprise client tables taught me the same lesson twice: a specialist who can explain a 0.02% dip in CTR isn't the same as a planner who can tell a client where growth is actually coming from.

Akash Puri
Account Manager, Display · Dentsu
February 4, 2025 · 2 min read
CM360ReportingAnalytics

Over nine-plus years on both sides of the media table — from execution floors in Pune to high-stakes reviews with global enterprise clients — I’ve sat through more meetings than I can count where a genuinely talented specialist gets lost in the weeds. The breaking point that made me want to write this down was a review for a global enterprise client where a junior team member spent fifteen minutes diagnosing a tiny fluctuation in click-through rate across static and video placements. I watched the client’s face the whole time. Total detachment. They didn’t care about a 0.02% shift in clicks — they cared about growth.

Here’s the read-order I use instead, top to bottom, every time a CM360 report lands on my desk.

1. Ignore the ghost metrics first (2 minutes)

Before anything else, I mentally grey out the columns that measure delivery rather than impact — raw impressions, raw clicks, whether the line item spent out smoothly. Useful for an ops check. Meaningless for a business read. High impressions mean nothing if the placements sat at the bottom of a page nobody scrolled to.

2. Check unique reach & frequency (5 minutes)

I don’t lead with total impressions in a client conversation — I lead with unique reach. Are we expanding the footprint to new potential buyers, or hammering the same audience ten times over and burning budget on repetition instead of expansion?

3. Check net viewable impressions (5 minutes)

Working with large global brands, brand safety and viewability aren’t optional. I look past total impressions to net viewable delivery, verified through DoubleVerify or IAS. A served impression nobody could physically see never had a chance to do anything.

4. Trace the Floodlight conversion path (10 minutes)

This is where the real read happens. I skip simple last-click attribution and look at the interplay between view-through conversions — people who saw the ad, didn’t click, but converted later — and click-throughs, to see how the whole ecosystem is actually working together.

5. Real example: the network that looked too good

Early in my career, one programmatic network on a B2B campaign looked like a clear winner on paper — cheap clicks, a click-through rate soaring above our premium direct placements. The ops instinct is to shift budget there immediately. Mapping the conversion paths against real pipeline data told a different story: those clicks were cheap because they were low quality, driven heavily by app placements with a near-zero post-click conversion rate. A seemingly “expensive” contextually targeted placement was quietly responsible for the real, high-value leads.

Wrapping up

By the end of this read, I have a one-page view that tells me:

  • Whether reach is actually expanding or just repeating.
  • Whether the impressions we paid for had a chance to be seen.
  • Which conversions are real versus attributed by accident of the last click.
  • Where the budget should move next.

A CM360 report shouldn’t just summarize where your money went. Read correctly, it tells you exactly where your next dollar needs to go.
